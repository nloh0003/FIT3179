Human Freedom Around The World

One particular method of measuring the state of human freedom is through the analysis of over 120 variables that all attribute to what is formally referred to as a Human Freedom Index (HFI). This universal measurement is based upon a myriad of factors ranging from personal, economic, religious freedom and many more. So which parts of the world demonstrate a 'satsifactory' level of freedom and what factors influence its HFI the most?

How does the Human Freedom Index vary around the world?

HFI is measured on a scale of 0 to 10, where 0 represents an absence of any freedom and a perfect score of 10 represents a utopia of civil liberty.

How has HFI changed over the years?

Between 2008 and 2017, the variance of Human Freedom has proven to be unique to each region. Regions such as the Middle East and North Africa have experienced decreasing levels of freedom where East Asia have shown to have increasing levels of freedom.
